The Elastic Clause Controversy

Article I, Section 8: "To make all Laws which shall be necessary and proper..."

"The Congress shall have Power... To make all Laws which shall be necessary and proper for carrying into Execution the foregoing Powers..."

How do you interpret "necessary and proper"?
üè¶ Real Example: National Bank (1791)

Hamilton's Question: Can Congress create a national bank?

  • Constitution says: Congress can coin money and regulate commerce
  • Doesn't say: Congress can create banks

üìö Elastic Clause

Also called: "Necessary and Proper Clause"

Location: Article I, Section 8

The Two Views:
üîµ Federalists (Hamilton):

"Necessary" = convenient or useful. This gives Congress flexibility to do whatever helps execute its powers.

üî¥ Democratic-Republicans (Jefferson):

"Necessary" = absolutely essential. Congress can only do what's explicitly listed in Constitution.

Why This Matters: This debate about federal power continues TODAY! Every argument about "big government vs small government" traces back to this.

December 1814: New England Federalists Meet in Secret

What's Happening:
  • War of 1812 is going badly
  • British burning Washington D.C.
  • New England economy devastated (no trade with Britain)
  • Federalists furious at Democratic-Republican war policy
The Federalists Propose:
1. Require 2/3 majority to declare war

Would make it harder for D-Rs to start wars

2. Limit presidents to one term

Stop Democratic-Republican dynasties (Jefferson ‚Üí Madison ‚Üí Monroe)

3. End three-fifths compromise

Stop South from getting extra representation from enslaved people

4. Require 2/3 majority to admit new states

Prevent Western states from diluting New England's power

‚ö†Ô∏è What Actually Happened:

Right as delegates arrived in Washington with their demands, news broke that:

  • Andrew Jackson won huge victory at Battle of New Orleans
  • Treaty of Ghent signed - war was over!

Result: Federalists looked unpatriotic for meeting during war. Party never recovered and died within a few years!

üìö Hartford Convention

When: Dec 15, 1814 - Jan 5, 1815

Where: Hartford, Connecticut

Who: Federalist delegates from New England

Why It Matters:

Marked the END of the Federalist Party as a national force.

The Timing Was Terrible:

They wanted constitutional amendments to limit federal power, but arrived in DC right when:

  1. War ended successfully
  2. Nation was celebrating victory

Made them look like traitors!

Connection: Federalists' opposition to federal power during war ironically killed their party, leaving D-Rs to expand federal power unchecked!
